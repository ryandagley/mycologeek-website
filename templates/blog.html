    Guess how loud a jack-hammer in your basement gets May 29, 2020

    Current Status:

    Over the last couple of days, we've had many plumbers, engineers, and an inspector in our basement. With that chaos and WFH, the days blend together more than ever before.

    While not necessarily a fun part of the project, Artemis Lab is located in my basement right next to where plumbers are jack-hammering to reline our sewer.

    While not needed to make progress in the lab, there was always a concern that our old deteriorating pipe would give up sometime in the next couple of years resulting in a flood that we've unfortunately dealt with in the past.

    Since this is a DIY site, here's some pointers:

    1. When you buy a home, have the inspector scope the sewer. Don't skip this. We did, and I shoveled human poop for a day and now feel suspicious in my basement.

    2. A repair like this is costly - we've been quoted between $13K and 18K for our repair. Many service companies offer financing at rates near 10% and some offer a discount if paying in full. As we've been saving for a while and called multiple plumbers, we are getting our repair at around $13k after a discount.

    As for how low we can go, I'm told that most sewer lines slope at .25" per foot toward the sewer. We're about 85 feet out.

    So... with a little bit of code: elevation_change = .25 * 85 we learn that our sewer is 21.25 feet deep. That doesn't seem right.
